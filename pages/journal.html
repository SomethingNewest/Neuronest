Why Keep a Brain Journal?

Research shows that self-monitoring cognitive function can lead to earlier detection of changes.

🔍 Spot Patterns Early

Daily tracking over months reveals trends invisible day-to-day. You might notice your sharpness is always lower after poor sleep or certain foods.

📋 Better Doctor Visits

Bring your journal data to appointments. Real data helps physicians understand your baseline and identify changes much more accurately than memory alone.

💪 Build Awareness

The act of reflecting on your cognitive state each day builds metacognitive awareness — a skill strongly associated with better memory performance in older adults.
